1. Overview

Course verification is a mandatory process to confirm that you have completed the required credit hours before proceeding to your internship placement. You must accumulate 130 credits to be eligible for internship.

2. Eligibility Requirements

  • You must be an active student in the InternLink system
  • You should have completed sufficient coursework
  • Your academic records must be up-to-date
  • You can only submit one application at a time

3. Document Preparation

3.1 Required Information

Your course verification document must include:

  • Student Information: Name, Student ID, Program, Academic Year
  • Course List: Complete list of courses taken with course codes
  • Credit Hours: Credit hours for each course
  • Total Credits: Sum of all completed credit hours
  • Grade/Status: Passing grade or completion status for each course

4. Document Content Guidelines

4.1 Sample Course List Format

Your document should contain a table or list similar to this:

Course Code Course Name Credit Hours Grade/Status
CS101 Introduction to Programming 3 A
CS201 Data Structures 4 B+
CS301 Database Systems 3 A-

4.2 Additional Recommendations

  • Include your official transcript if available
  • Attach course registration forms if applicable
  • Provide academic advisor's stamp or signature if required
  • Include semester/year information for each course
  • Clearly highlight the total credit count

5. Submission Process

  1. Log in to the InternLink system
  2. Navigate to the Course Verification page
  3. Click "Apply for Verification" button
  4. Enter your current total credit count
  5. Upload your prepared document
  6. Review all information carefully
  7. Submit your application
‚úÖ Submission Checklist:
  • Document includes all required course information
  • Credit count is accurate and matches the total in document
  • File is in acceptable format (PDF/DOC/Image)
  • File size is under 10 MB
  • Document is clear and readable
  • All pages are included (if multi-page)

6. After Submission

6.1 Review Process

After you submit your application:

  • Your application status will show as "Pending"
  • A lecturer will review your submission
  • Review typically takes 3-5 business days
  • You will receive an email notification when reviewed

6.2 Possible Outcomes

Status Meaning Action Required
Pending Under review Wait for lecturer's decision
Approved Verification successful Proceed to next steps (placement application)
Rejected Verification unsuccessful Review lecturer's remarks and resubmit

6.3 If Rejected

If your application is rejected:

  1. Read the lecturer's remarks carefully - They will explain why it was rejected
  2. Correct the issues identified in the remarks
  3. Prepare an updated document addressing all concerns
  4. Resubmit your application with the corrected information
‚ö†Ô∏è Important Notes:
  • You can only have one pending application at a time
  • You cannot apply again once approved
  • You can edit or delete your application while it's pending
  • After rejection, you can submit a new application
